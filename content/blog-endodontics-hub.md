# Endodontics: Evidence-Based Guides on Root Canal Treatment and Dental Pulp Care

## What Is Endodontics?

Endodontics is **the branch of dentistry concerning the dental pulp and tissues surrounding the root of a tooth**. Endodontists are dental specialists who complete 3 or more years of additional university training beyond a general dental degree. General dentists perform basic root canal treatments, but they refer complex cases involving multiple canals, curved roots or surgical needs to endodontic specialists. Specialists must maintain registration with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA) and the Dental Board of Australia.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does an endodontist do?

An endodontist is **a dental specialist who diagnoses and treats conditions affecting the dental pulp and tissues inside and around the tooth root**. They perform root canal treatment, endodontic surgery such as apicoectomy, retreatment of failed root canals and management of dental trauma. Endodontists complete an additional 3 years of specialist university training after their general dental degree.

### How is an endodontist different from a general dentist?

General dentists perform routine root canal treatments, particularly on front teeth with simple canal anatomy. Endodontists are **specialists who handle complex cases involving multiple canals, curved roots, retreatment of failed procedures and surgical endodontics**. General dentists refer complex cases to endodontists. Both general dentists and endodontists must hold current registration with AHPRA and the Dental Board of Australia.

### Do I need a referral to see an endodontist?

In most cases a general dentist refers patients to an endodontist for complex cases, but **a referral is not legally required in Australia**. Patients can contact an endodontic clinic directly. However, a general dentist holds prior clinical records and X-rays that the endodontist needs, making a referral the optimal pathway for continuity of care.

### How much does endodontic treatment cost in Australia?

Root canal treatment in Australia ranges from approximately $900 for a single-canal front tooth to $3,000 or more for a multi-canal molar treated by a specialist endodontist. **Private health insurance Extras cover typically reimburses a portion of the cost under item numbers 312 to 318**. Medicare does not cover endodontic procedures for adults.

### What should I do if I think I need a root canal?

If you experience severe localised toothache, sensitivity to hot or cold that lingers, pain on chewing or swelling near a tooth, see a dentist promptly. **These symptoms indicate pulp infection that requires immediate physical intervention**.
